About the Programs

What Makeful offers and how each format works.

Makeful runs three program formats: Teacher Professional Development, Summer Camp and Full-Year Enrichment. Each is designed for a different context, but all combine FashionTech design, 3D printing and hands-on making.

What exactly is FashionTech STEAM?

expand_more

FashionTech STEAM uses fashion design as a way into serious technical learning: 3D printing, materials science, electronics, AI design tools and sustainability. Students work on real challenges, like designing garments that purify air or monitor stress levels, and learn engineering and technology through making something they actually want to wear.

STEAM stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, Art and Mathematics. Fashion gives those subjects a real, tangible context that makes them feel meaningful rather than abstract.

What are the three program formats and how do they differ?

expand_more

Teacher Professional Development, A self-paced online course that equips teachers with the skills, confidence, and physical kit to bring FashionTech STEAM into their own classrooms. No prior technical knowledge needed. Includes 25 modules, 70+ video tutorials, and a complete 3D-printed fashion kit shipped to the teacher.

Summer Camp Program, A complete 14-day program licensed to schools. Makeful trains your school's staff to deliver it. All hardware and materials are provided by Makeful. Schools run the sessions themselves with Makeful's full curriculum support.

Full-Year Enrichment Program, Bi-weekly online sessions run by Makeful-qualified instructors, in small groups of maximum 8 students. A school guardian is present at all sessions. Students build a full project portfolio over the academic year. Hardware is delivered to and managed by Makeful.

Does Makeful send instructors to schools?

expand_more

No. For Summer Camp and short-term programs, Makeful trains the school's own staff to deliver the program. You use your own people, with Makeful's curriculum, materials and support behind them.

For the Full-Year Enrichment Program, Makeful instructors run all sessions online. They never visit the school in person. A school guardian is always present in the online session.

What age group are the programs designed for?

expand_more

Makeful programs are designed for K–12 students. Specific program tracks are age-appropriate and will be confirmed at enrollment. The Summer Camp and Full-Year Enrichment programs work best with students aged 14–19. Teacher Professional Development is for adults.

Students do not need prior experience with technology, 3D printing or fashion design. Curiosity is the only requirement.

What do students actually make during the program?

expand_more

Students tackle real-world design challenges and build physical, wearable projects. Examples include:

  • 3D-printed garments that purify the air. One student's ballet dress won awards for this.
  • Stress-monitoring wearables using conductive filament
  • Glow-in-the-dark safety clothing for high-pollution cities
  • Thermochromic accessories that change colour with temperature
  • Redesigned medical wearables combining function with beauty

Every student leaves with at least one finished, portfolio-quality piece. Full-Year students build a complete portfolio of projects.

How are online sessions structured for the Full-Year Program?

expand_more

Sessions run every two weeks in small groups of up to 8 students. Each session is led live by a Makeful instructor via video call, with the school guardian present throughout. Sessions combine direct instruction, hands-on making time at students' workstations, and group discussion or feedback.

Between sessions, students can access the Esmerise platform for resources, tutorials and the student forum. The school guardian can see all activity there.

Is the program available in languages other than English?

expand_more

Programs are currently delivered in English, with Czech also available. We are expanding to other languages. Contact us if you have a specific language requirement and we will do our best.

In which countries are programs available?

expand_more

Makeful is based in Prague, Czech Republic, and works with schools globally. We have a strong focus on English-speaking markets including the US and UK, and currently work with schools in more than 8 countries.

The Full-Year Enrichment Program can run online from any location. For Summer Camp programs, hardware shipping will be confirmed at enrollment.

Section 2

Hardware & Equipment

Everything about the 3D printers and materials we provide.

Makeful is fully responsible for all hardware. Schools never need to buy, repair, or replace any equipment. Here's how it all works.

Does the school need to buy any equipment?

expand_more

No. Makeful provides all 3D printers, tools, filaments and materials needed to run the program. Everything is included in the program fee. Schools provide the venue, power outlets, Wi-Fi and their own staff or guardian. That is it.

There are no hidden material costs or equipment surcharges. What we quote is what you pay.

What happens if a 3D printer breaks during the program?

expand_more

Makeful fixes it or replaces it at no cost to the school. All hardware is owned, insured and maintained by Makeful. Schools are never responsible for repair or replacement costs during normal program use.

If a printer stops working during the program, contact us and we will arrange a repair or replacement as quickly as possible.

How does the hardware get to our school and back?

expand_more

Makeful arranges delivery of all hardware to your school before the program starts and collects it after it ends. Delivery logistics are confirmed at enrollment. Makeful handles international shipping to the UK, US and other markets. Schools do not need to arrange anything.

What do we need to provide in terms of space and facilities?

expand_more

You need a dedicated workspace with:

  • Adequate power outlets, at minimum one per 3D printer
  • Reasonable ventilation. 3D printing produces minimal fumes but good airflow is recommended.
  • Tables at a comfortable working height for students
  • A lockable storage space for hardware when not in use
  • Reliable Wi-Fi for platform access and online sessions

We'll send you a detailed venue checklist after enrollment so you can confirm everything is in place before the program starts.

Are the materials safe for children to use?

expand_more

Yes. All materials are selected with student safety in mind. Standard PLA filament, the most common material used, is made from plant-based sources and is one of the safest 3D printing materials available. We do not use materials that require fume extraction beyond normal room ventilation.

Specific material safety data sheets are available on request. Please inform us of any known allergies at enrollment so we can advise accordingly.

Section 3

For Schools & Coordinators

Procurement, logistics, and what to expect.

The questions school coordinators and procurement teams typically ask before signing up. If something is not covered here, email us and we will respond within 2 working days.

How do we get started?

expand_more

Request an info pack via our contact form or email [email protected]. We'll come back to you with full program details, pricing, and a proposed timeline. If you'd like to discuss further before committing, we're happy to arrange a call with Linette directly.

How much does it cost?

expand_more

Pricing depends on the program format, number of students and your location. We do not publish a fixed price list because we want to quote accurately for your specific situation. Shipping hardware internationally, for example, varies significantly.

Request an info pack and we will send you a detailed proposal with transparent pricing. No hidden costs. What we quote covers everything including hardware, materials and training.

What staff training does Makeful provide for Summer Camp?

expand_more

Full training for your designated staff is included in the Summer Camp fee. It covers operating the 3D printers safely, delivering the curriculum modules, managing the schedule, troubleshooting common hardware issues and supporting students through the design and making process.

Training is delivered online ahead of the program. Staff do not need any prior experience with 3D printing. We have designed the training to take them from zero to confident.

Can we include Makeful as an extracurricular activity in our school prospectus?

expand_more

Yes, and we actively encourage it. We can provide approved descriptions, logos and imagery for your school prospectus, website or marketing materials. Contact us for a media pack.

What does the school guardian need to do during online sessions?

expand_more

The school guardian must be present in the video call for the full duration of every session. Their role is supervisory. They do not need to teach or facilitate, but they must be visible and present. This is a firm safeguarding requirement. If a guardian cannot attend, the school must notify Makeful at least 24 hours in advance and Makeful will reschedule.

Does the program meet curriculum standards?

expand_more

Makeful programs are designed to complement and enrich school curricula, not replace specific lessons. They cover learning outcomes across Design and Technology, Science, Computing, Art and Design, and Maths, depending on the program and age group. We can provide curriculum mapping documentation on request.

Is there a minimum or maximum number of students per program?

expand_more

For the Full-Year Enrichment Program, groups are capped at 8 students. This is intentional. Small groups allow for proper mentorship, full access to hardware and the focused creative work that produces real portfolio pieces.

For Summer Camp programs, group sizes are specified in the program agreement. Minimum enrollment thresholds apply and will be confirmed at the proposal stage.

Section 4

For Teachers

About the professional development program.

Teacher training is central to everything Makeful does. Here is what it looks like from the teacher's perspective.

Do I need technical experience to join the teacher training?

expand_more

No. Linette has spent 7 years working out what works with 3D printing in education, so you do not have to. The training is designed to take you from zero to confident, regardless of your technical background. If you can teach, you can do this.

What's included in the teacher professional development course?

expand_more

The course includes:

  • 25 modules with 70+ video tutorials, all self-paced on Esmerise
  • 8 talks from working FashionTech designers and innovators
  • Ready-to-use teaching materials you can bring directly to your students
  • Mentoring and support from Linette throughout the course
  • A complete physical 3D-printed fashion kit with materials, shipped to you

Once complete, you will have a licence to use the teaching materials in your own classroom.

How long does the teacher training take?

expand_more

The course is fully self-paced. Most teachers complete it over 4 to 8 weeks depending on how much time they can give it. There is no expiry on access, so you can take longer if you need to.

Can I run Makeful-style sessions in my classroom after completing the training?

expand_more

Yes, that is exactly the point. Your training includes a personal classroom licence to use Makeful's teaching materials and lesson plans with your own students. The licence is personal to you and covers use within your own teaching.

If you want to run a wider school program, host a summer camp or build something bigger, talk to us. We are happy to discuss a school-level licence.

Will I get a certificate on completion?

expand_more

Yes. Teachers who complete the program receive a certificate of completion. We are also working on formal CPD accreditation for future cohorts. Contact us for the latest.

Section 5

For Students & Parents

What participating in a Makeful program is actually like.

What students and parents most often ask before joining a Makeful program.

My child has no experience with technology. Is Makeful right for them?

expand_more

Yes. Students who come in curious but inexperienced often have the best experiences, because nothing gets in the way of their excitement. You do not need to know what a 3D printer is to learn how to use one. You just need to want to make something.

Is online communication between my child and a Makeful instructor supervised?

expand_more

Yes, always. Makeful has strict supervision rules for all communication with students:

  • All live sessions have a school guardian present in the call
  • Email communication always includes the school guardian in copy
  • Written communication on the Esmerise platform is visible to all participants and the guardian. There are no private messages.
  • Makeful instructors will never contact students through social media, personal messages or any channel outside these supervised routes

For full details, see our Safeguarding & Child Protection Statement.

Who owns the things my child makes?

expand_more

Your child owns everything they make. Makeful makes no claim over student work, designs or portfolios. The physical project goes home with the student.

Makeful may ask permission to photograph student work for our website or marketing materials. This is always separate, always optional and always requires written consent from the parent or guardian.

Will my child receive any marketing emails from Makeful?

expand_more

No. Student email addresses are only used for enrollment confirmation and online class access. We never send students newsletters, promotional emails or any marketing content. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

What if my child misses a session?

expand_more

For the Full-Year Program, sessions are recorded and available on the Esmerise platform. Missing one session will not derail a student's project. The curriculum has some built-in flexibility. If a student misses several sessions, the school and Makeful will work together to find the best way forward.

For Summer Camp sessions delivered by school staff, catch-up arrangements are at the school's discretion.

Still have questions?

We'll answer everything personally.

No automated responses. No generic brochures. Just a real conversation about whether Makeful is right for your school.
